## Why

GitHub issue: https://github.com/zsoltkulcsar/blog/issues/7

The first Keskeny Út prototype shows the visual direction, but the MVP must prove a complete partner-presentable publication: real Reformed/Protestant content, readable article pages, clear navigation, CMS-backed publishing direction, and a realistic path to serve thousands of readers. This planning change turns the prototype into an implementation-ready MVP plan for pastors, writers, translators, and ministry partners.

## What Changes

- Define a presentable MVP for a Hungarian Christian editorial magazine focused on content quality and trustworthy outlook.
- Add real reader journeys: homepage, article detail pages, topic browsing, series browsing, author/translator context, and basic search/discovery direction.
- Define an MVP content set using real or near-real Hungarian articles, including translated article workflows inspired by sources such as Desiring God and The Gospel Coalition.
- Define CMS and database expectations for article writing, review, publishing, translation metadata, categorization, and future scale.
- Keep newsletter signup visual-only for MVP while leaving a clear later integration path.
- Define partner-presentation readiness: clean design, usable navigation, polished sample content, deployment readiness, and a scale story for thousands of readers.
- Keep non-editorial church-platform features out of scope: events, donations, sermons, member accounts, and church administration.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `presentable-mvp`: Covers the full MVP requirements for presenting Keskeny Út to pastors, writers, translators, and partners.

### Modified Capabilities
- `editorial-blog-planning`: Extends the archived homepage-first planning with MVP-level content, CMS/database, translation, reader discovery, and presentation-readiness requirements.

## Impact

- OpenSpec planning artifacts under `openspec/changes/plan-presentable-mvp/`.
- GitHub issue #7 tracks the overall MVP planning change.
- Implementation will affect routing, article detail pages, content modeling, CMS/database integration planning, editorial workflow, navigation, SEO, deployment, and CI.
- Expected product direction is a real MVP, not only a visual prototype.
- The MVP can start with static/seeded content during implementation, but must be designed around CMS-backed article writing and database persistence.
